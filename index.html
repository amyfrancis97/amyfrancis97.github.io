About me

Where biology meets
machine learning.

Patient, precise, and always asking the harder question.

I'm a Senior Data Scientist and first hire at Nexus BioQuest, where I established the data science function from scratch — building analytical infrastructure, consulting with clients across pharmaceutical and biotech sectors, and developing tools that make complex data accessible to scientists.

My PhD at the University of Bristol, funded by a competitive Cancer Research UK studentship, focused on predicting the functional impact of genetic variants in cancer genomes. I've since worked at Roche in Basel and Zürich, exploring protein language models and antibody optimisation.

Beyond the code, I've led interdisciplinary teams to back-to-back hackathon victories — at Cambridge and the Wellcome Collection — and co-organised Bristol's first AI in Health meeting, securing two interdisciplinary research grants. I believe the best science happens at the edges of disciplines, and I love building the teams and environments that make that happen.
